How We're Reviving Myanmar's Parks

Transforming neglected green spaces into vibrant community hubs through ecological restoration, cultural preservation, and active community participation.

URGENT NEED

The Critical Need to Revitalize Myanmar's Local Parks

Myanmar's urban green spaces are in crisis. With only 0.41m² of park space per person - less than 5% of the WHO standard - our communities are deprived of essential green infrastructure. Parks are more than just open spaces; they are vital for public health, environmental sustainability, cultural preservation, and social cohesion.

Our Three-Pillar Approach to Park Revitalization

Holistic transformation through interconnected focus areas

Environmental Restoration

Rebuilding native ecosystems, enhancing biodiversity, and implementing sustainable water management to create resilient urban green spaces that combat climate change and improve air quality.

  • Native plant species reintroduction
  • Sustainable water management systems
  • Urban heat island reduction
  • Wildlife habitat creation
  • Soil health improvement

Cultural Integration

Weaving Myanmar's rich heritage, traditional arts, and community identity into park design to create spaces that celebrate our history, tell our stories, and preserve cultural traditions.

  • Public art by local artists
  • Historical interpretation
  • Cultural events and festivals
  • Traditional craft workshops
  • Indigenous knowledge preservation

Community Engagement

Empowering residents through participatory design, volunteer programs, and inclusive activities that ensure parks serve everyone equitably and become true community assets.

  • Community design workshops
  • Volunteer maintenance programs
  • Intergenerational activities
  • Accessibility improvements
  • Youth leadership programs
