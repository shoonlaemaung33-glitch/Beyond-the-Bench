Park Photography Tips - Session Details

What You'll Learn

  • Essential camera settings for outdoor photography
  • Composition techniques for stunning landscape shots
  • How to capture wildlife without disturbing natural habitats
  • Lighting strategies for different times of day
  • Post-processing tips for mobile photography
  • How to prepare for photo competitions

About the Host

Ma Sandar Kyaw is an award-winning wildlife photographer with over 15 years of experience. Her work has been featured in National Geographic and she has won multiple international photography competitions.

Session Agenda

  • 7:00 PM - Introduction & Overview
  • 7:15 PM - Technical Photography Basics
  • 7:45 PM - Composition Masterclass
  • 8:15 PM - Live Q&A Session
  • 8:45 PM - Closing Remarks

Required: None, but having a camera or smartphone is recommended

Skill Level: Beginner to Intermediate

Materials Provided: Presentation slides and resource guide

Sustainable Park Practices Workshop - Session Details

What You'll Learn

  • Leave-No-Trace principles for park visitors
  • Sustainable hiking and camping practices
  • How to minimize your carbon footprint in parks
  • Wildlife protection and ethical observation
  • Community conservation initiatives
  • How to advocate for park sustainability

About the Hosts

The Environmental Conservation Team consists of park rangers, environmental scientists, and conservation activists who have been working together for over a decade to protect natural spaces.

Session Agenda

Required: None

Historical Park Stories - Session Details

What You'll Discover

  • Indigenous history of local park lands
  • Historical landmarks within parks

  • Cultural significance of natural features
  • How history shapes current park use

Dr. Michael Chen is a historian specializing in local history and cultural heritage. He has published several books on regional history and regularly contributes to historical preservation projects.

Upcoming Events & Announcements

Stay updated with our community activities and events calendar

Community Cleanup Day

Date: March 12, 2026

Time: 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Location: Mya Nandar Park

Details: Join us for a morning of park cleanup and restoration. Gloves, bags, and tools will be provided. All ages welcome! This is a family-friendly event where we'll work together to remove litter, clear trails, and plant native species. Refreshments and snacks will be served after the cleanup. Register in advance to help us plan for supplies.

Expected Participants: 100+ community members

Special Feature: Eco-friendly workshop on recycling

Cultural Storytelling Evening

Date: March 18, 2026

Time: 6:30 PM - 8:30 PM

Location: Thakin Mya Park

Details: Elders from local Indigenous communities will share traditional stories about the land, parks, and natural world. This immersive evening includes storytelling, traditional music, and cultural demonstrations. Family-friendly event with traditional snacks and crafts. Learn about the deep connection between people and parks through oral history.

Featured Storytellers: 5 community elders

Special Feature: Traditional craft demonstration

Park Art Competition Exhibition

Submission Deadline: March 30, 2026

Exhibition Dates: April 10-17, 2024

Location: City Art Gallery & Online Virtual Tour

Details: Submit artwork inspired by parks in three categories: Painting, Photography, and Digital Art. Winners will receive cash prizes and their work will be featured in a special exhibition. All submitted works will be displayed in our online gallery. Opening night includes artist talks and award ceremony.

Prizes: $1,000 total prize money

Special Feature: Virtual reality exhibition tour

Spring Birdwatching Workshop

Date: April 3, 2026

Time: 7:00 AM - 10:00 AM

Location: Kandaw Mingalar Park

Details: Learn to identify spring migratory birds with expert ornithologists. Perfect for beginners and experienced birdwatchers alike. Binoculars and field guides provided. We'll cover identification techniques, bird behavior, and conservation efforts. Hot drinks and breakfast snacks served. Dress warmly and bring your camera!

Guides: 3 expert ornithologists

Special Feature: Rare bird species spotting
